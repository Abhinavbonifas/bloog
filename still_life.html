Still Life Photography

Still life photography is the art of capturing inanimate objects in a carefully arranged composition, emphasizing their form, texture, and color. Everyday items like flowers, food, and personal belongings are often used to create visually striking or symbolic images. The genre allows photographers complete control over lighting, shadows, and arrangement, enabling them to craft a particular mood or narrative. Soft, controlled lighting is commonly used to enhance details, while reflections and shadows add depth to the composition. Rooted in traditions of still life painting, especially from the Dutch Golden Age, this genre continues to thrive in both commercial and fine art photography, offering endless opportunities for creativity and self-expression.
